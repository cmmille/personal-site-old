About Me

I am a plant biologist, recently turned software developer. I first started learning programming as a necessity in graduate school but fell in love with programming's unique combination of problem solving and creativity. When I'm not on my computer, I can be found wandering the miscellaneous trails of Fort Collins in search of birds.

My Skills

Plant Biology

I am interested in the evolutionary relationships in plants. My M.S. work studied the reproductive barriers in wild tomato species. I am experienced with a wide array of molecular techniques including RNAseq, Western Blots, Flow Cytometry, and more.

Software Development

Computers have been an important part of my life since I first started modding computer games as a kid. In school, I first learned Python, R, and the Unix command line to process data and run statistics. Three years ago, I took on the role of frontend developer for my lab's MS Access Database. I recently earned my Certificate of Computer Programming and am currently enrolled in different Web Development courses through Udemy.

I am comfortable working with a wide variety of platforms and languages from Visual Basic to Java (including Android and Spring) to Web Development (HTML/CSS/JavaScript).

Wildlife Photography
